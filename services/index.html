What Gets Delivered

Concrete Outputs at Every Tier

Engagements aren't open-ended. Each tier produces specific deliverables — written documents, working systems, and decisions you can act on.

01

Technology Strategy

Starting with how the business actually operates — the commercial model, the growth levers, the constraints — then defining a technology direction that genuinely supports it. The board sees a roadmap they can trust because it's anchored in the business, not in the tech.

02

Architecture & Platform Design

Assessing your current architecture, identifying risk and debt, and mapping an evolutionary path — whether that's moving from monolith to microservices, a cloud migration, or scaling for 10× load.

03

Engineering Team Leadership

Building, mentoring, and where necessary restructuring your engineering function — instilling the practices, delivery culture, and hiring pipelines that compound over time.

04

Delivery Pipeline & Cadence

Introducing pragmatic delivery frameworks — sprint discipline, CI/CD pipelines, incident management — that remove friction and help teams ship with genuine confidence.

05

Technical Audits & Due Diligence

Rigorous, independent assessment of codebases, infrastructure, security posture, and engineering process — delivered in a format that works for founders and investors alike.

06

Investor & Board Readiness

Translating complex technical realities into clear, commercial narratives that founders, boards, investors, and potential acquirers can actually act on — and making sure the right things are being measured. No jargon, no theatre.

No lock-in, no notice periods, no awkward exits

The whole point of fractional engagement is flexibility. The principles that govern every contract:

  • → Exit at the end of any phase. No notice periods, no severance clauses.
  • → Audit fees credit against retainers. If you proceed to a retainer within 60 days of an audit, the audit fee comes off the first month.
  • → Scale up or down as the business evolves. Retainer days can flex during critical sprints or board-facing milestones.
  • → Capability transfer is the goal. The point of every engagement is that you can run the system without me afterwards.
